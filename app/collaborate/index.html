About us

The Library Innovation Lab is a team of librarians, programmers, artists, and lawyers with a broad mission: to build open source tools and services, open to everyone and in the public interest, solving serious emerging problems from the unique perspective of the world's largest academic law library. On the tech side we work most often with Django, Vue, and Postgres.

Web Developer

While we encourage anyone to apply, our Web Developer roles are a particularly good fit for experienced developers who want to spend some time working in the public interest in an academic setting, with room to pursue their own priorities relating to our mission.

What we offer

  • Work on stuff that matters. Our core projects are about opening access to law; redefining legal casebooks and reducing their cost; and exploring cutting-edge web archival techniques to keep important links working for the next 100 years.
  • Build open source. All of our projects are open source and otherwise designed to maximize the public benefit of our work.
  • Set your own pace. We are a small, self-directed group. Pick the priorities where you see the highest impact at a pace that feels comfortable. We have real vacations and no crunch time.
  • Set your own research agenda. We are located at the Harvard Law School Library and share a kitchen with the Berkman Klein Center and metaLAB. Going to events, having conversations, developing your scholarly perspective, and exploring it through code are encouraged. We will specifically prioritize these things at the cost of shipping the next feature.
  • Have job stability and support. While we maintain the creative atmosphere and flexibility of a small innovation lab, we're backed by an established institution with strong benefits and support for employees.

Location

We are based in a newly renovated space on the Harvard Law School campus. While you can set your own schedule for home and on-site days, the ideal candidate will benefit from regular on-site collaboration with our team and the Harvard community.

Technologist in Residence

In addition to the web developer role posted above, the Library Innovation Lab is seeking a technologist-in-residence to join our team on a one-year appointment.

This is a full-time, salaried term appointment intended for senior software engineers who are looking for a year to take stock at Harvard before beginning the next phase of their careers. You will:

  • Join our developer team meetings.
  • Advise on architecture, strategy, and process.
  • Take on challenging software implementation tasks at the core of our projects.
  • Design your own research agenda, which may include exploring, coding, writing, and speaking related to the future of the internet, law, libraries, or education, based on your existing experience and interests.

In short, we offer an experienced software engineer a year for contemplation and exploration, housed at the Harvard Law School Library, in exchange for helping us to enhance our software quality and public impact.

Applications will be considered on a rolling basis beginning February 28, 2022. Start date will be individually negotiated.
